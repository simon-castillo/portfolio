Tfl & the London Transport Museum

A 5 day sprint to find ways of attracting visitors and new Londoners to Santander Cycles

My role: UX Designer in a multi-disciplinary design team

Mapping user journeys and pinpointing problems

The team began by understanding the Santander Cycle Hire scheme from business and user perspectives. After gaining insights from TfL and interviewing the general public, we mapped the user's journey through the service, including common pain points at each stage.

Mapping user journeys and pinpointing problems

My initial concept had two key parts. The first was a dedicated Cycle Hire Ambassador located at selected docking stations, who would assist new users and give pre-booked safety inductions in the form of a short ride around the local area.

The second was an add-on to the Cycle Hire mobile app, which suggested interesting routes near each docking station. These could be viewed by difficulty and/or subject (e.g. museums, architecture, parks).

User generated content

After testing various prototypes with the public and receiving feedback from the TfL experience team, the sprint team developed the idea of user generated cycle routes. How can we build on the natural tendency of users to share experiences online? Can shares on social media be used to suggest new routes? Is social media a more attractive entry point to the app? It was important to not only design the new feature but also how users will discover and return to it.

​

The full concept was presented to the wider TfL team at the end of the sprint, suggesting new avenues for innovation of the app and wider Cycle Hire scheme.
