Designing for accessibility

Brief

Create a website for accessibility guidance, based on the popular ‘Designing for Accessibility’
posters created by Karwai Pun.

View the work in progress

Making the guidance itself accessible

This project aims to bring important design advice to a wider audience.

As a first version, Megan Rodger and I have created pages that closely mirror the original poster layouts. After jumping into coding the desktop version, we stepped back, designed for mobile first and worked upwards in resolution, concentrating on essential functionality and considering how users would navigate the site or interact with the guidance.

Making the most of digital

But this project is also a chance to act on feedback and expand on the content in the posters. What makes a sentence or colour scheme ‘simple’ and why is that preferable? What is ‘good’ contrast?

So for the second iteration we are focusing on designing for this new content, with the help of content designers and access needs experts around the Home Office.

Opening up for feedback

From presenting our work around government, we’ve had some great ideas for future changes. One suggestion is that the site could be shown to stakeholders when making the case for accessibility, so detailed case studies might be added to encourage empathy with people’s struggles.

We are completely open to feedback or collaboration with people in or outside government, so please feel free to get in touch.
