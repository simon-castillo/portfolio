EnergyWiser

Brief

Refine the existing desktop on-boarding process, from comparing energy
tarrifs to registration and switching suppliers.

Understanding energy

First, the team gained as much understanding of the home energy market as possible, looking at everything from suppliers, market regulators, government advice pages and the latest news. We also systematically reviewed competitor sites to find best-in-class patterns as well as opportunities for improvement.

Empathising with energy shoppers

Collectively, UK households are overcharged by £2.9bn per year by being on the wrong tariff - so why don't people switch? We produced a survey to ask people about their experiences and gained deeper insights by interviewing selected individuals.

Designing for users, with stakeholders

After analysing the research, I hosted a structured workshop for rapid idea generation and collaborative design to use the client's subject matter expertise together with our knowledge of users.

Testing and iterating prototypes

​Three rounds of user testing and iteration were completed, with the fidelity increasing between each cycle. Once the concept and flow were validated, we moved into a lo-fi clickable prototype and finally a full mock-up of the actual pages.

Writing content to build trust

​Users repeatedly told us of their lack of trust in energy suppliers. So, we made transparency a priority. There are concise definitions for jargon and explanations for how each piece of their information will be used.

To take the hassle out of switching, tariff results are given as soon as a good estimate is possible, based on information that people can recall easily e.g. the size of their house and number of occupants. They can save those results and increase the accuracy when they have time to find an old energy bill.
