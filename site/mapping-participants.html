Mapping research participants

Helping Home Office user research to be inclusive, so that our products and services work for all users.

Original brief

Help user researchers to determine a research participant’s Digital Inclusion category and record it in a transparent way.

Measuring inclusion

The digital inclusion (DI) scale is a means to measure an individual's digital skills, attitude and access to digital. It has 9 points, ranging from those that ‘never have and never will’ go online to those who have ‘expert’ skills such as coding. By mapping research participants to the scale, researchers on product teams can spot any gaps and adjust their research to be more representative of their product’s users.

This project aimed to make the mapping process more consistent between Home Office researchers and provide a higher level, transparent view of how inclusive Home Office user research is.
