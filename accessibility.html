Designing for accessibility

Brief

Create a website for accessibility guidance, based on the ‘Designing for Accessibility’
poster series created by Karwai Pun. View the work in progress.

Making guidance more accessible

This project aims to bring important design advice to a wider audience.

As a first version, I have worked with another designer to build pages that closely mirror the original poster layouts. We designed for mobile first and worked upwards in resolution, considering how users might navigate the site or interact with the content differently in a digital format.

Making the most of digital

But this is also a chance to act on feedback and expand on the content in the posters. What makes a sentence or colour scheme ‘simple’ and why is that preferable? What is ‘good’ contrast?

So for the second iteration we are focusing on designing for this new content, with the help of content designers and access needs experts around the Home Office.

Opening up to new contributors

From presenting our work around government, we’ve had some great ideas for future changes. For example the site could be shown to stakeholders when making a case for accessibile design, so detailed case studies might be added to encourage empathy with users' struggles.

The work in progress is open source and live on Github pages. We are completely open to feedback or collaboration with people in or outside government, so please feel free to get in touch.
